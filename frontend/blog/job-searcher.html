Building a Job Alert System While Unemployed: A Reality Check

TL;DR (Too Long; Didn't Read): I spent 12 hours building an AWS serverless job searcher because I couldn't afford to spend half my day job hunting while studying for my AWS certification with a toddler who demands my full attention and counting every dollar.

January 2026 15 min read AWS Projects

The Problem

I've been unemployed for 16 months. I have a toddler. My husband works at Starbucks. We're counting every dollar.

Every morning, I'd wake up and start searching Indeed, LinkedIn, Dice, scrolling through hundreds of jobs. But here's the thing: with a toddler, you can't just block out "2 hours for job hunting."

Jobs post throughout the day. Your kid needs snacks. Wants you to play princesses. Demands your FULL attention during potty training (no diapers, we're doing this). You search for 20 minutes, get interrupted for "Mommy, come HERE right NOW," come back 2 hours later, lose your place, start over.

And honestly? I'm blessed to spend this time with my toddler. The toddler bosses me around, and that's fine. But it also means job hunting takes 4 to 5 hours spread across the entire day instead of a focused 2 hour block.

Most of those jobs? Required active TS/SCI clearance I don't have. Were in Maryland (60+ mile commute). Paid below what I need (has to cover more than just daycare costs). Were senior roles I'm not qualified for. Were scams or MLM schemes.

And even if you save a job search on popular job search engines? It throws in anything with "engineer" at the end. Search for "cloud engineer" and you'll get network engineer, software engineer, sales engineer, mechanical engineer. Search for "cloud support engineer" and it's the same mess. The filters don't actually filter.

By the end of the day, I was mentally exhausted. Studying for my AWS Solutions Architect certification? Forget it. I was done.

Something had to change.

The Solution (In Theory)

Build an automated job alert system that searches for cloud/DevOps jobs in Virginia, filters out clearance requirements, Maryland locations, and senior roles, emails me relevant jobs at 7 AM daily, and costs $0/month (because we're counting every dollar).

Stack: Lambda (Python), DynamoDB (job tracking), SNS (email alerts), EventBridge (scheduler), CloudFormation (infrastructure as code).

Simple, right?

Reality: 12 Hours of "Simple"

Hour 1 to 2: CloudFormation Setup

I started with the infrastructure. Four CloudFormation stacks: DynamoDB tables (jobs + seen jobs with 90 day TTL), SNS topic for email notifications, Lambda function, EventBridge scheduler.

First mistake: Used the wrong email in my SNS stack. Had to delete and redeploy. CloudFormation deletes take 2 to 5 minutes. Cool.

Second mistake: SNS topic ARN was wrong in Lambda environment variables. Spent 20 minutes debugging why emails weren't sending, only to realize I'd copied a Lambda runtime ARN instead of the SNS topic ARN.

YAY ME. Just keep making mistakes. It's fine. Everything's fine.

Hour 3 to 4.5: The Indeed RSS Disaster

My original plan: Use Indeed's RSS feeds. They're free, public, and return fresh jobs.

Plot twist: Indeed's RSS feeds don't work anymore. Or they do, but not reliably. The URLs are rss.indeed.com (not www.rss.indeed.com), but even then: "This page can't be reached. DNS_PROBE_FINISHED_NXDOMAIN"

Tried for over an hour. Nothing. Searched documentation. Tried different parameters. Tried the old www.indeed.com/rss format. Nope.

Turns out Indeed is phasing out RSS in favor of requiring paid API access in 2025.

Great.

Hour 4.5 to 6: Enter SerpAPI (And More Problems)

SerpAPI scrapes search engines for you. They have a free tier: 100 searches/month.

Spent 1.5 hours integrating it, configuring the Lambda function, testing, and getting errors.

Status Code 400: {'error': 'Unsupported `indeed` search engine.'}

Wait, what?

Checked my SerpAPI account dashboard. Zero searches used. It wasn't even reaching the API.

Kept testing. Kept checking. Finally realized: Indeed engine isn't supported on free tier. Requires $50/month.

We're counting every dollar. $50/month for job alerts? Absolutely not.

Problem 2: Switched to Google Jobs engine (free tier). Had to completely rewrite the Lambda function to handle Google's job structure instead of Indeed's.

Problem 3: Environment variables. API keys. Making sure I'm not hardcoding secrets. This is VERY important: never put API keys in your code. Using AWS CLI to set Lambda env vars instead.

Meanwhile: Toddler wants to play dolls. Right now. With my full attention. Husband is at work. I'm running on fumes.

Hour 6 to 8: DynamoDB Schema Mismatch

Finally got SerpAPI working with Google Jobs. Lambda was fetching jobs!

Error: Missing the key job_id in the item

My code was using 'id' but DynamoDB expected 'job_id'. Had to update every reference in the code, repackage the Lambda zip, redeploy.

Then the dependencies got wiped out. Had to reinstall feedparser and requests. Repackage. Redeploy.

Then it happened again because I kept forgetting to include the dependencies in the zip.

Three. Separate. Times.

YAY ME AGAIN.

Job 3: Senior Principal Cloud Engineer. Me: I'm not principal level. Rejected.

Job 4: DevOps Engineer, Chantilly, VA. Me: Looks perfect!

Me: Great.

The Clearance Problem

Here's the thing: Google Jobs only shows job snippets. The clearance requirements? Those are buried in the full job description on the company's website.

My Lambda function can't see them. It can only filter based on what Google returns.

So I'll still get false positives.

But you know what? It's still better than manually searching Indeed throughout the day while a toddler bosses me around. Now I can check my email at 7 AM, Ctrl+F "clearance" on each job, skip the ones that require active clearance, and apply to the rest.

10 minutes instead of half my day.

Hour 9 to 12: Deploy the Scheduler

Final step: Deploy the EventBridge scheduler so it runs daily at 7 AM EST.

aws cloudformation create-stack --stack-name job-alerts-scheduler --template-body file://infrastructure/04-scheduler.yaml --region us-east-1

Wait for it to complete.

Done.

IT'S ALIVE.

The Final Stats

What I built: 4 CloudFormation stacks, 1 Lambda function (Python, ~250 lines), 2 DynamoDB tables, 1 SNS topic, 1 EventBridge scheduler, smart filtering for location, seniority, and clearance keywords.

Cost: $0/month (all free tier)

Time invested: 12 hours (spread across one very long day)

Time saved per week: ~15+ hours

ROI: Break even after 1 week

What I Learned

1. Free tier limitations are real. SerpAPI's free tier doesn't support Indeed. Had to pivot to Google Jobs.

2. DynamoDB schema mismatches will destroy you. 'id' vs 'job_id' cost me 30 minutes and three redeployments.

3. You can't filter what you can't see. Google Jobs snippets don't include clearance requirements. False positives will happen.

4. Environment variables over hardcoding secrets. Use aws lambda update-function-configuration to set env vars. Never put API keys in code. This is VERY important.

5. Dependencies in Lambda packages are easy to forget. If your zip is 7KB instead of 1.3MB, you forgot to include feedparser and requests. Ask me how I know.

6. Testing in production is real. Sometimes you just have to deploy it and see what breaks. Then fix it. Then redeploy. Three times.

Either the filters are now too strict, or the DC Metro area really doesn't have that many cloud engineering jobs without clearance requirements. Probably both.
